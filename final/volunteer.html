Become a Volunteer

Volunteering at our community garden is a rewarding experience that connects you with nature and your community.

As a volunteer, you'll learn about plant care, sustainable gardening practices, and environmental conservation.

Plus, you'll meet fellow gardening enthusiasts and contribute to a cause that beautifies our neighborhood and supports local biodiversity.

Volunteer Roles and Responsibilities

We offer various roles for our volunteers, catering to different interests and skills.

Whether you enjoy hands-on gardening, leading workshops, or helping with community events, there's a place for you.

Our volunteers help with planting, maintenance, organizing events, and more.

Volunteer Roles

  • Planting
  • Maintenance
  • Workshop Leader
  • Event Organizer

How to Join

Joining our volunteer team is easy! Fill out our volunteer application form. We welcome individuals of all skill levels.

After submitting your application, our volunteer coordinator will contact you to discuss your interests and availability.

We also host monthly volunteer orientation sessions, where you can learn more about the garden and how you can contribute.

Application Process

Becoming a volunteer at Green Thumb Community Garden is a straightforward process:

  1. Visit our website's volunteer section and access the application form.
  2. Fill out the form with your personal details, gardening experience, and areas of interest.
  3. Submit the application online. You will receive a confirmation email upon submission.
  4. Our volunteer coordinator will review your application and contact you to discuss further steps.
Orientation Sessions

To acquaint new volunteers with the garden and its operations, we organize monthly orientation sessions:

  • Session Overview: Introduction to the garden's layout, ongoing projects, and volunteer roles.
  • Meet and Greet: Opportunity to meet fellow volunteers and coordinators.
  • Q&A Session: Address any queries you might have about volunteering.
  • Tour: Guided tour of the garden, highlighting key areas and projects.

Note: Orientation sessions are held on the first Saturday of every month. Please register in advance to attend.
