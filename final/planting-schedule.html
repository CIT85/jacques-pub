When to Plant and Harvest

Timing is everything in gardening.

Our schedule is crafted to align with nature's rhythm. In spring, as the soil warms up, it's ideal for planting vegetables like tomatoes and peppers, and flowers like roses and tulips.

Summer is our reward season, with gardens bursting in colors and flavors. Autumn is the time to plant hardy winter crops and to prepare the beds for next year's growth.

Monthly Gardening Tips

Each month brings its unique tasks and joys.

January and February are for planning and ordering seeds. March sees the start of indoor sowing. April and May are busy with transplanting seedlings outdoors.

The summer months are for maintenance and enjoying the bounty, while autumn focuses on harvesting and winter preparations.

Monthly Gardening Insights

  • January-February: Planning and ordering seeds. A quiet time in the garden, perfect for planning the year ahead.
  • March: Start of indoor sowing. Begin sowing seeds indoors to get a head start on the growing season.
  • April-May: Transplanting seedlings outdoors. As the frost passes, it's time to move young plants outside.
  • June-August: Maintenance and harvesting. Enjoy the fruits of your labor and keep the garden thriving.
  • September-October: Harvesting and preparing for winter. Collect the last of the harvest and ready the garden for colder months.
  • November-December: Winter care. Protect sensitive plants and plan for next year.
